Overview

Borongan is a city located on the eastern coast of Samar Island in the Eastern Visayas region of the Philippines. It is the capital of Eastern Samar province and is known for its beautiful beaches, rich history, and vibrant culture.

Geography

Borongan has a total land area of 544.3 square kilometers and is bordered by the Pacific Ocean to the east, the municipality of Balangkayan to the south, the municipality of Lawaan to the west, and the municipality of Maydolong to the north. The city has a hilly terrain, with the highest point being Mount Hilong-Hilong, which has an elevation of 1,332 meters.

Tourism

Borongan is a popular tourist destination, with its beautiful beaches, historical landmarks, and cultural attractions. Some of the must-visit places in Borongan include the Borongan Cathedral, the St. Lawrence the Martyr Parish Church, the Pawikan Conservation Center, and the Magasang Falls.

Visitors can also enjoy various water activities, such as surfing, swimming, and snorkeling, in the city's pristine beaches, including the popular Malajog Beach and the rocky Anahawan Beach.

Economy

Borongan's economy is primarily based on agriculture, fishery, and tourism. The city is known for its production of copra, banana, and rice, as well as its abundant supply of seafood, such as fish, shrimp, and crab. The city also has a thriving furniture industry, with several furniture factories located in the area.

Tourism is also a significant contributor to Borongan's economy, with the city attracting both local and foreign visitors. The city has several hotels, resorts, and restaurants that cater to tourists' needs. The city government is also actively promoting Borongan as a tourist destination, with various tourism projects and programs.
